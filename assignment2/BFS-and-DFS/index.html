About Jeddah

Jeddah is a vibrant city located on the coast of the Red Sea in Saudi Arabia. It is the second-largest city in the country and serves as a major commercial hub.

"Jeddah, where history and modernity collide, is a delightful city that captures hearts. Located on the beautiful Red Sea coast, it effortlessly blends Saudi Arabian heritage with a warm and welcoming atmosphere. With stunning architecture, charming streets, and mouthwatering cuisine, Jeddah offers an enchanting experience through its rich culture. From serene shores to lively markets, Jeddah's charm is simply irresistible."

Culture

Islamic Heritage

ddah, like the rest of Saudi Arabia, has a strong Islamic heritage. Islam plays a significant role in the daily lives of people in Jeddah, and the city is home to numerous mosques, including the iconic King Fahd Fountain Mosque and the historic Al Rahmah Mosque.

Traditional Architecture

Jeddah's historic district, known as Al-Balad, showcases traditional architecture with its coral houses, intricate wooden balconies, and narrow winding streets. Al-Balad is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers a glimpse into Jeddah's cultural past.

Art and Culture

Jeddah has a vibrant art and cultural scene. The city is known for its modern and contemporary art exhibitions, galleries, and public art installations. The Jeddah Sculpture Museum, Athr Gallery, and the Jeddah Art Week are some of the prominent venues and events that showcase artistic expression in the city.

Festivals and Events

Jeddah hosts various cultural festivals and events throughout the year. The Jeddah Season is a major entertainment and cultural festival that attracts both locals and tourists. It features music concerts, theatrical performances, art exhibitions, and a range of activities catering to different interests.

Cuisine

he food culture in Jeddah is diverse and reflects the culinary traditions of Saudi Arabia and the wider Middle East. Local dishes such as Kabsa (a spiced rice dish with meat), Mandi (a traditional rice and meat dish), and Jareesh (a porridge-like dish made from crushed wheat) are popular. Jeddah also offers a wide range of international dining options.

Traditional Souks

Jeddah is known for its bustling traditional souks, or markets, where visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture. Souq Al-Alawi and Souq Al-Nada are famous for their vibrant atmosphere and the sale of traditional goods such as spices, textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts.
